Global Hunger and Food Security Crisis

The World Food Programme (WFP) is the leading humanitarian organization saving lives and changing lives, delivering food assistance in emergencies, and working with communities to improve nutrition and build resilience.

With **345 million people** worldwide facing acute food insecurity, WFP’s efforts are more critical than ever. From war zones to climate-disaster regions, we provide life-saving food assistance and sustainable solutions to end world hunger.

Emergency Relief

103 MILLION people reached by WFP through emergency assistance in 2023.

19 MILLION women and children supported by WFP malnutrition prevention and treatment programmes in 15 emergencies in 2023.

24 HOURS was the average time for WFP to reach people with emergency support in 47 crises in 2023.

WFP’s Impact Across the World

Since its founding in 1961, WFP has provided life-saving assistance to millions of people affected by conflicts, disasters, and climate shocks. With over 5,600 trucks, 30 ships, and 100 aircraft in action daily, we ensure food reaches those who need it most.

WFP works in over **120 countries**, partnering with governments, local communities, and NGOs to tackle hunger, improve food systems, and support school meal programs for children.
